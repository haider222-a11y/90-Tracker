Core instruction
Start by fixing the worldview lens
Study existentialism and Sartre not to accept them, but to understand the modern materialist mindset that says humans create their own meaning. Compare that with Islam, where humans are created with purpose, responsibility, and accountability.
Study: Existentialism, Jean-Paul Sartre, “existence precedes essence,” freedom, bad faith, responsibility, meaning without God.
Write notes: How does Islam answer Sartre’s claim that man creates his own meaning?
Islamic foundation
Amanah before advanced knowledge
The Amanah discussion is the moral foundation: knowledge without responsibility is dangerous. Before studying powerful phenomena, understand human limits, arrogance, ignorance, obedience, and accountability.
Study: Surah Al-Ahzab 33:72, tafsir of Amanah, faith vs survival, human limitation.
Write notes: What kind of person is safe to carry powerful knowledge?
Reality model
Reality may be layered, not only material
Study Plato and Plotinus as philosophy training. Use them to understand how thinkers imagined levels of reality, soul, intellect, and higher/lower existence. Do not treat Greek philosophy as Islamic aqidah; compare it carefully.
Study: Plato’s Forms, Allegory of the Cave, Plotinus, Enneads, higher intelligences.
Write notes: Which ideas help thinking beyond materialism, and which must be rejected Islamically?
Science tools
Information, coding, decoding, and systems
Study biology, microbiology, AI, signal processing, and communication systems because they teach how information can be encoded, transmitted, decoded, and reconstructed. This is an analogy and language for careful thinking, not proof of teleportation.
Study: DNA, gene expression, signal processing, AI translation, encoding/decoding, animal communication.
Write notes: What is a real scientific mechanism, what is only a conceptual analogy?
Phase 1 — Existentialism & Sartre
Mind
Goal: understand modern thinking that rejects unseen realities, fixed purpose, and divine order.
Videos: Crash Course Philosophy — Existentialism; The School of Life — Sartre.
Read online: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Existentialism; Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Existentialism; IEP/SEP on Sartre.
Books: Thomas Flynn — Existentialism: A Very Short Introduction; Sartre — Existentialism Is a Humanism; Sartre — Nausea; advanced later: Being and Nothingness.
Notes: Define existentialism, existence precedes essence, freedom, bad faith, anxiety, and the Islamic response: purpose before existence.
Phase 2 — Islamic Worldview & Amanah
Aqidah lens
Goal: understand human responsibility before studying powerful knowledge or Quranic advanced phenomena.
Quran: Surah Al-Ahzab 33:72; focus on Amanah, ẓalūm, jahūl.

Notes: What is Amanah? Why are humans described as unjust and ignorant? What is the danger of knowledge without accountability?
Phase 3 — Plato, Plotinus & Levels of Reality
Reality
Goal: train the mind to think beyond sensory materialism while staying careful with Islamic boundaries.
Plato: The Republic — Cave and Forms; Phaedo — soul and immortality; Timaeus — cosmology later.
Plotinus: SEP/IEP Plotinus; selected Enneads; Lloyd P. Gerson — Plotinus; O’Meara — Plotinus: An Introduction to the Enneads.
Notes: What does it mean that reality has levels? How is this only philosophical training, not Islamic proof by itself?
Phase 4 — Biology, Microbiology & Quranic Communication
Life as info
Goal: understand life as organized information and study natural communication examples linked to Quranic reflection.
Biology: Khan Academy DNA structure/function, gene expression, DNA sequencing; Campbell Biology; Alberts — Molecular Biology of the Cell.
Quran examples: Bees — Surah An-Nahl 16:68–69; ants — Surah An-Naml 27:18–19; birds — Surah An-Naml 27:16.
Animal communication: Honeybee waggle dance, ant pheromones, animal cognition, bioacoustics and machine learning.
Notes: How does information organize life? What is communication in nature beyond human language?
Phase 5 — AI, Coding/Decoding & Signal Processing
Systems
Goal: learn the technical language of encoding, transmission, decoding, pattern recognition, and reconstruction.
AI: Harvard CS50 AI with Python; Russell & Norvig — Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach; Chollet — Deep Learning with Python.
Signals: MIT OpenCourseWare Signals and Systems; MIT 6.003 YouTube playlist; Oppenheim & Willsky — Signals and Systems; Haykin — Communication Systems.
Notes: Explain input → encoding → transmission → decoding → output. Separate analogy from evidence.
Phase 6 — Surah An-Naml 27:38–40
Application
Goal: return to the throne of Bilqis carefully after building philosophical, Islamic, and scientific language.
Quran: Surah An-Naml 27:38–40 — jinn, the one with knowledge of the Book, and Sulayman’s gratitude.
Tafsir: Quran.com tafsir, Ibn Kathir, Maududi/Tafhim al-Qur’an, Ma‘arif al-Qur’an, The Study Quran.
Questions: Who had knowledge of the Book? Why was knowledge faster than jinn strength? Was it physical movement, metaphysical means, or Allah’s direct command? Why did Sulayman say this is from the grace of my Lord?
Notes: Do not begin by claiming technology. Begin with tafsir, humility, divine permission, and what is proven vs unknown.
